# Strategic Self-Advocacy (SSA™) — Public Overview

This short summary explains the SSA™ approach students should use as the project’s user-centred frame.

## What is SSA™?

Strategic Self-Advocacy (SSA™) is an approach that treats advocacy, creative work and lived-experience documentation as **forms of authorship**. SSA™ emphasises the person (or group) authoring the material, their agency over how materials are used, and governance that preserves that authorship through time.

## Core principles

- **Authorship-first** — records should make it clear *who* authored them and in what role.  
- **Consent-centred** — metadata must capture consent scope, limitations and any withdrawal.  
- **Accessibility-by-default** — outputs should be accessible (Easy-Read, AAC, captions, Auslan scripts where relevant).  
- **Human accountability** — AI outputs require explicit human review; humans retain final judgement.  
- **Traceability** — provenance and version history must be preserved so records remain auditable.

## Why SSA matters for this project

- SSA shapes what metadata is essential, what AI may safely do, and what governance is required. Students must show how their proposals strengthen SSA outcomes (preserving agency, improving accessibility, and preventing re-traumatisation).
